The Woodcutter and the Hare

Zen Story

There was once a woodcutter working in the forest, chopping down trees. Suddenly, he noticed a hare across a clearing watching him, and he thought, "I am going to catch that animal and eat it for lunch." But the hare could read his thoughts, and said, "you think you're going to catch me, don't you?" The woodcutter charged at the hare, but it disappeared immediately into the bushes.

A few moments later, the hare appeared again at the opposite end of the clearing, laughing. "You can't catch me!" it sneered. So the woodcutter devised a plan, and thought to himself, "next time I see that animal, I'm going to move to the opposite side of the clearing to which it appears." The animal responded, "you're thinking of moving to the opposite side of the clearing to which you see me appear, aren't you?"

The hare kept playing back and forth with the woodcutter. For over an hour, the man tried to tactically run in a variety of different directions to trap the hare, all to no avail. Eventually, he gave up and returned to chopping trees. The animal laughed at him, "you've given up!" Just that moment, the axe head flew off the axe, and killed the hare.
